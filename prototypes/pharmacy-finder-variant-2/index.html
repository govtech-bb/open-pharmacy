If your medicine is not listed

Ask your doctor:

  • if there is another listed medicine you can use
  • if they can ask for special approval

Special approval is only for government pharmacy use.

What prescription colours mean

Your prescription colour can help show which pharmacy can fill it.

Where you can use your prescription also depends on where it came from.

Blue prescription

This is usually from a public polyclinic or outpatient clinic.

You can take it to:

  • a government pharmacy
  • a participating private pharmacy

At a participating private pharmacy, you pay a fee to prepare your medicine. This is called a dispensing fee.

Yellow or green prescription

This is usually from the Queen Elizabeth Hospital.

You can take it to:

  • the Queen Elizabeth Hospital pharmacy
  • some government pharmacies for selected medicines

If you take it to a participating private pharmacy, you may have to pay the full cost.

Pink or white prescription

This is usually from a private doctor.

You can take it to:

  • a government pharmacy
  • a participating private pharmacy

At a participating private pharmacy, you pay a fee to prepare your medicine. This is called a dispensing fee.

Some medicines may still cost money.

If you are not sure

Ask the pharmacist to check before you pay.

Report wrong pharmacy details

Pharmacy details can change.

Tell the Barbados Drug Service if:

  • an address is wrong
  • a phone number is wrong
  • opening hours are wrong
  • a pharmacy has closed
  • a pharmacy should be added
  • a map pin is in the wrong place

Email: management@drugservice.gov.bb

Include:

  • the pharmacy name
  • what needs to be changed
  • your name and contact details, if you want a reply

You can also call the Barbados Drug Service.

Phone: (246) 535-4300

What if there is a problem at the pharmacy?

Use this page to find out what to do if there is a problem at the pharmacy.

Your ID was not accepted

Ask the pharmacist:

  • why the ID was not accepted
  • what ID they need
  • to check again if you think the ID should be accepted

The pharmacy asked you to pay more than expected

Before you pay, ask the pharmacist to check if you should get help with the cost of your medicine.

If you already paid:

  • keep your receipt
  • write down the medicine name, pharmacy name and amount you paid
  • then contact the Barbados Drug Service

The pharmacy is out of stock

Ask the pharmacist:

  • when the medicine may be available
  • if another pharmacy has the medicine
  • if they can help you find where to go next

Contact the Barbados Drug Service

Phone: (246) 535-4300

Email: management@drugservice.gov.bb

Find a pharmacy for Barbados Drug Service prescription medicine

This service helps you find pharmacies where you can use the Special Benefit Service (SBS).

SBS is a Barbados Drug Service programme that helps lower or remove the cost of some prescription medicines.

You can search for:

  • government pharmacies
  • private pharmacies that work with the Barbados Drug Service

Some medicines may still cost money.

Who can get help with medicine costs

Barbadian citizens, permanent residents and people covered by CARICOM free movement can get help with medicine costs if they are:

  • under 16
  • 65 or over
  • any age and need medicine for diabetes, asthma, high blood pressure, cancer, epilepsy or glaucoma

They must also have:

  • a prescription from a doctor registered with the Medical Council of Barbados. The doctor can work in the public or private health service.
  • a prescription medicine on the Barbados National Drug Formulary

The Barbados National Drug Formulary is the list of medicines covered by the Barbados Drug Service.

What you may pay

Some medicines may still cost money.

  • At a government pharmacy, you do not pay a fee to prepare your medicine.
  • At a participating private pharmacy, you pay a fee to prepare your medicine. This is called a dispensing fee. The amount depends on the medicine.

Prescription colour

Your prescription colour can affect which pharmacies can fill it.

What to take to the pharmacy

  • the prescription
  • original accepted ID for the person the medicine is for

Accepted ID can be:

  • Barbados National Identification (ID) card
  • Barbados passport
  • Barbados driver’s licence
  • child health book, if the medicine is for a child

Photocopies are not accepted.

Need help at the pharmacy?

You may need help if:

  • your ID is not accepted
  • the pharmacy asks you to pay more than expected
  • the pharmacy is out of stock

You can also contact the Barbados Drug Service.

Phone: (246) 535-4300

Email: management@drugservice.gov.bb
