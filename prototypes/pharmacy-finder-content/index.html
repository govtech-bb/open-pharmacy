Where you can use your prescription

Prescriptions may be different colours.

The colour can help show which pharmacy can fill it.

Blue
From a public polyclinic or health centre. A government or a private pharmacy can fill it.
Pink
From a private doctor. A government or a private pharmacy can fill it.
Yellow or green
From a Queen Elizabeth Hospital outpatient clinic. Only a government pharmacy can fill it.

Check if your medicine is listed

The Barbados National Drug Formulary is the list of medicines covered by the Barbados Drug Service.

You can search it to check if your medicine is on the list.

Who can get medicine through the Barbados Drug Service

You may be able to get medicine through the Barbados Drug Service if you:

  • are a Barbadian citizen
  • are a permanent resident
  • are covered by CARICOM free movement

You must also have a prescription for a medicine on the Barbados National Drug Formulary.

The prescription must be from a doctor registered with the Medical Council of Barbados.

Your doctor can work in the public or private health service.

Choose a pharmacy type

Government pharmacies

Government pharmacies only provide medicines covered by the Barbados Drug Service.

Some medicines may still cost money.

You do not pay a fee to prepare your medicine.

Participating private pharmacies

These are private pharmacies that work with the Barbados Drug Service.

They may have longer opening hours.

They may sell other medicines and items you can buy without a prescription.

You pay a fee to prepare your medicine. This is called a dispensing fee.

What to take to the pharmacy

Take:

  • the prescription
  • original accepted ID for the person the medicine is for

Accepted ID can be:

  • Barbados National Identification (ID) card
  • Barbados passport
  • Barbados driver’s licence
  • child health book, if the medicine is for a child

Photocopies are not accepted.

What you may pay

Some medicines may still cost money.

  • At a government pharmacy, you do not pay a fee to prepare your medicine.
  • At a participating private pharmacy, you pay a fee to prepare your medicine. This is called a dispensing fee.
