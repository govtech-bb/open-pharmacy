Participating private pharmacies

These are private pharmacies that work with the Barbados Drug Service.

They may have longer opening hours.

They may sell other medicines and items you can buy without a prescription.

You pay a fee to prepare your medicine.

Show participating private pharmacies

What you may pay

Some medicines may still cost money.

The Barbados Drug Service may help lower the cost of some medicines.

At participating private pharmacies, you pay a fee to prepare your medicine. This is called a dispensing fee.

Prescriptions may be different colours.

What to take to the pharmacy

Take:

  • the prescription
  • accepted ID for the person the medicine is for

Accepted ID means a Barbados National Identification (ID) card, Barbados passport or Barbados driver’s licence.

For a child, a child health book is also accepted.

Take the original ID. Photocopies are not accepted.

Who can get help with medicine costs

The Barbados Drug Service may help lower the cost of some medicines.

This can depend on the person’s age or health condition.
