Find a pharmacy open now

Last updated: June 2026

Use the Special Benefit Service (SBS) to collect prescription medication from a Barbados pharmacy. There is no dispensing fee at a government pharmacy. At a Private Participating Pharmacy (PPP) you pay a dispensing fee. The Barbados Drug Service (BDS) reimburses the pharmacy on your behalf. Some items are not covered.

Find an open pharmacy

Not sure where to take your prescription? Check which pharmacy you can use.

Need help? Call the Barbados Drug Service on (246) 535-4300.

Find the right pharmacy

You cannot use every pharmacy for every prescription. The colour of your prescription paper tells you whether a government pharmacy, a private pharmacy, or both can fill it.

1. Check which pharmacy can fill your prescription

Blue
From a public polyclinic or health centre. A government or a private pharmacy can fill it (a dispensing fee applies at a private pharmacy).
Pink
From a private doctor. A government or a private pharmacy can fill it (a dispensing fee applies at a private pharmacy).
Yellow or green
From a Queen Elizabeth Hospital outpatient clinic. Only a government pharmacy can fill it — not covered at private pharmacies.

2. If both can fill it, choose government or private

  • Government pharmacy — no dispensing fee. Formulary drugs only.
  • Private pharmacy (PPP) — a dispensing fee applies, but there are more locations with evening and weekend hours, and they also stock non-formulary and over-the-counter products. Look for the SBS logo.

Check the Barbados National Drug Formulary to see which items are covered for the current period.

3. Find one open near you

You do not have to use the pharmacy in your parish.

Blue or pink prescription: find a pharmacy that's open now (government or private).

Yellow or green prescription: find a government pharmacy that's open now.

Check if you can use this service

You can use the Special Benefit Service if you are:

  • under 16 years old
  • 65 years or over, or
  • any age and need formulary drugs (medicines on the Barbados National Drug Formulary, called Benefit Drugs) for diabetes, asthma, hypertension, cancer, epilepsy or glaucoma

You can have seen your doctor in either the public or the private sector.

What you need to bring

You can use this service if you show a valid Barbados ID card or passport in person and you are:

  • a Barbadian citizen
  • a permanent resident, or
  • participating in the free movement of citizens from CARICOM countries

You must also have a prescription for a formulary drug from a doctor registered with the Medical Council of Barbados. Present your ID and prescription to the pharmacist at any of the pharmacies listed.

What you pay

There is no dispensing fee at a government pharmacy. At a Private Participating Pharmacy (PPP) you pay a dispensing fee of $7, $10 or $14 per item, depending on the drug (rates set on 1 April 2024).

Not all items are free. You may be required to pay for some items in both the private and government sectors, for example if:

  • you do not meet the conditions above
  • the item is a contraceptive
  • the item is a repeat of certain medicines, such as allergy medicines
  • two drugs fall into the same therapeutic category
  • the quantity exceeds the maximum allowed free by the BDS

How to get your medication

  1. 1

    What to bring

    You will need a valid Barbados ID card or a valid Barbados passport. Photocopies of ID cards are not accepted.

    If you are a permanent resident, also bring proof of permanent residency — your passport stamped by Barbados Immigration, or a Permanent Resident's certificate from the Barbados Immigration Department.

    If you are collecting for a child, bring the child's Green Card (and birth certificate or ID), your own ID, and your permanent residency documents if applicable. If you are not the parent, bring a letter of guardianship.

    If your stamp is in an old passport, bring both old and new passports. If your new passport has no stamp, visit Immigration at BTI Corporate Centre, Princess Alice Highway, Bridgetown BB11093, St. Michael first.

  2. 2

    Get a prescription

    A prescription can come from a public-sector doctor (polyclinic or Queen Elizabeth Hospital) or a private doctor. The colour of your prescription affects where you can use it — check which pharmacy you can use.

  3. 3

    At the pharmacy

    1. Hand your prescription and ID documents to the pharmacist.
    2. The pharmacist will check your eligibility against the Barbados Drug Service register.
    3. If you are eligible, the pharmacist will give you your medication. You must sign a reimbursement form to acknowledge receipt and enable the pharmacy to be reimbursed by the BDS.
    4. At a Private Participating Pharmacy you pay the dispensing fee. You may also be required to pay for some items at both government and private pharmacies (see What you pay).

    If your documents are not accepted, do not pay full price. Ask the pharmacist to call the Barbados Drug Service on (246) 535-4300 first.

If something goes wrong

My drug is not on the formulary
Ask your doctor about a therapeutically equivalent alternative on the Barbados National Drug Formulary. If no equivalent exists, your doctor can request a Category B exception through the Drug and Therapeutics Committee at Queen Elizabeth Hospital, or through the Barbados Drug Service Director. This only applies in the government setting. The BDS does not honour Category B requests from private doctors.
My ID was not accepted
Ask the pharmacist to contact BDS on (246) 535-4300 before you pay. If your permanent residency documentation needs updating, contact the Immigration Department at BTI Corporate Centre, Princess Alice Highway, Bridgetown BB11093, St. Michael.
I was charged full price but think I qualify
Keep your receipt. Contact BDS on (246) 535-4300 or email management@drugservice.gov.bb with your prescription, receipt and ID documents. You may be entitled to a refund.
How are dispensing fees and prices set?
The dispensing fee paid by the patient is contractually set, and all PPPs are bound to charge patients according to this pricing schedule for items dispensed to beneficiaries under the SBS program. The same pricing schedule applies in government pharmacies. Non-formulary items, and formulary items dispensed to non-beneficiaries, are priced according to each pharmacy’s own business model.
My child just turned 16
The under-16 benefit ends on the child's 16th birthday. If the child has a qualifying chronic condition (asthma, diabetes, etc.), the benefit continues under that category, because the drug and the patient's age determine qualification.
The pharmacy is out of stock
The pharmacy should direct you to another participating pharmacy. If they cannot help, call BDS on (246) 535-4300.
Collecting on someone else's behalf
Medication must be collected in person. This service is not available online. Someone else can collect for you. They will need to bring your prescription, your ID, your permanent residency documents, and their own ID.

Tell us about a change

Pharmacy details can change. If you spot an out-of-date address, phone number or opening hours, a closure, a pharmacy that should be added, or a map pin in the wrong place, let the Barbados Drug Service know so we can keep this service accurate.

Email management@drugservice.gov.bb or call (246) 535-4300. Please include the pharmacy name and what needs to change.
