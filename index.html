Find a pharmacy open now

Last updated: May 2026

Use the Special Benefit Service (SBS) to collect prescription medication from a Barbados pharmacy — free at a government polyclinic, or for a small dispensing fee at a private SBS pharmacy. The Barbados Drug Service (BDS) pays the pharmacy on your behalf.

Find an open pharmacy

Need help? Call the Barbados Drug Service on (246) 535-4300.

Check if you can use this service

You can use the Special Benefit Service if you are a Barbadian citizen or permanent resident and a doctor has given you a prescription on the Barbados National Drug Formulary.

Who gets the lowest price

You pay nothing at a government polyclinic pharmacy, and only the dispensing fee at a private SBS pharmacy, if you are:

  • aged 65 or over
  • under 16 years old
  • prescribed medication for asthma, cancer, diabetes, epilepsy, glaucoma or hypertension (any age)

Everyone else

If you do not fall into one of the groups above, you can still collect your medication free at a government polyclinic pharmacy. At a private pharmacy you will pay the full price.

How to get your medication

  1. 1

    What to bring

    You will need your Barbados ID card, plus one of the following to prove permanent residency:

    • Your passport stamped by Barbados Immigration confirming permanent residency
    • A Permanent Resident's certificate from the Barbados Immigration Department

    If you are collecting for a child, bring the child's birth certificate or ID, your own ID, and your permanent residency documents. If you are not the parent, bring a letter of guardianship.

    If your stamp is in an old passport, bring both old and new passports. If your new passport has no stamp, visit Immigration at Careenage House, The Wharf, Bridgetown first.

  2. 2

    Get a prescription

    A prescription can come from a public-sector doctor (polyclinic or Queen Elizabeth Hospital) or a private doctor.

    Find out which prescription colour you have

    The paper colour tells you where the prescription came from and where it can be used.

    Blue
    From a public polyclinic or health centre. Free at a government pharmacy, dispensing fee only at a private SBS pharmacy.
    Pink
    From a private doctor. Free at a government pharmacy, dispensing fee only at a private SBS pharmacy.
    Yellow or green
    From a Queen Elizabeth Hospital outpatient clinic. Free at a government pharmacy. Not covered at private pharmacies, so you will pay full price. Ask your hospital doctor to reissue on a standard form if you want to use a private pharmacy.
  3. 3

    Choose where to go

    Government polyclinic pharmacy: medication is completely free. 12 locations across the island. You do not have to use the polyclinic in your parish.

    Private SBS pharmacy: a small dispensing fee applies, but there are more locations with evening and weekend hours. Use the directory to find one near you.

  4. 4

    At the pharmacy

    1. Hand your prescription and ID documents to the pharmacist.
    2. The pharmacist will check your eligibility against the Barbados Drug Service register.
    3. If you are eligible, the pharmacist will give you your medication.
    4. At a government pharmacy you pay nothing. At a private SBS pharmacy you pay the dispensing fee only.

    If your documents are not accepted, do not pay full price. Ask the pharmacist to call the Barbados Drug Service on (246) 535-4300 first.

If something goes wrong

My drug is not on the formulary
Ask your doctor about a therapeutically equivalent alternative on the Barbados National Drug Formulary. If no equivalent exists, your doctor can request a Category B exception through the Drug and Therapeutics Committee at Queen Elizabeth Hospital, or through the Barbados Drug Service Director.
My ID was not accepted
Ask the pharmacist to contact BDS on (246) 535-4300 before you pay. If your permanent residency documentation needs updating, contact the Immigration Department at Careenage House, The Wharf, Bridgetown.
I was charged full price but think I qualify
Keep your receipt. Contact BDS on (246) 535-4300 or email director@drugservice.gov.bb with your prescription, receipt and ID documents. You may be entitled to a refund.
My child just turned 16
The under-16 benefit ends on the child's 16th birthday. If the child has a qualifying chronic condition (asthma, diabetes, etc.), the benefit continues under that category. Ask the doctor to show the qualifying condition on future prescriptions.
The pharmacy is out of stock
The pharmacy should direct you to another participating pharmacy. If they cannot help, call BDS on (246) 535-4300.
Collecting on someone else's behalf
Medication must be collected in person. This service is not available online. Someone else can collect for you. They will need to bring your prescription, your ID, your permanent residency documents, and their own ID.
