Get free or subsidised medication

Last updated on June 10th, 2026

Use the Special Benefit Service (SBS) to collect prescription medication from a Barbados pharmacy. There is no dispensing fee at a government pharmacy; you pay one at a Private Participating Pharmacy (PPP). The Barbados Drug Service (BDS) pays the pharmacy for covered medicines — some items are not.

Not sure where to take your prescription? Check which pharmacy you can use.

Need help? Call the Barbados Drug Service on (246) 535-4300.

Check if you can use this service

You can use the Special Benefit Service if you:

  • are a Barbadian citizen, a permanent resident, or part of the CARICOM free movement of citizens, and
  • have a prescription on the Barbados National Drug Formulary from a doctor registered with the Medical Council of Barbados

Your doctor can be public or private.

Find an open pharmacy

Know your prescription

Not every pharmacy can fill every prescription. The colour of your prescription paper tells you which can — and you do not have to use the pharmacy in your parish.

Blue
From a public polyclinic or health centre. A government or a private pharmacy can fill it. Find a pharmacy open now.
Pink
From a private doctor. A government or a private pharmacy can fill it. Find a pharmacy open now.
Yellow or green
From a Queen Elizabeth Hospital outpatient clinic. Only a government pharmacy can fill it — not covered at private pharmacies. Find a government pharmacy open now.

If both can fill it, choose government or private

  • Government pharmacy — no dispensing fee. Formulary drugs only.
  • Private pharmacy (PPP) — a dispensing fee applies, but there are more locations with evening and weekend hours, and they also stock non-formulary and over-the-counter products. Look for the SBS logo.

Check the Barbados National Drug Formulary to see which items are covered for the current period.

What to bring

Bring a valid Barbados ID card or passport. Photocopies are not accepted.

Permanent residents: also bring proof of residency — a passport stamped by Barbados Immigration, or a Permanent Resident's certificate.

Collecting for a child: bring the child's Green Card (and birth certificate or ID), your own ID, your residency documents if applicable, and a letter of guardianship if you are not the parent.

If your residency stamp is in an old passport, bring both. If your new passport has no stamp, visit Immigration at BTI Corporate Centre, Princess Alice Highway, Bridgetown BB11093, St. Michael first.

What you pay

There is no dispensing fee at a government pharmacy. At a Private Participating Pharmacy (PPP) you pay a dispensing fee, and what you pay depends on whether you qualify for the lowest price.

Who gets the lowest price

At a Private Participating Pharmacy you pay only the dispensing fee — not the full price of the medicine — if you are:

  • aged 15 or under
  • 65 years or over, or
  • any age and prescribed formulary drugs (Benefit Drugs) for diabetes, asthma, hypertension, cancer, epilepsy or glaucoma

Everyone else

If you are not in one of the groups above, you pay the full price at a Private Participating Pharmacy. A government pharmacy still has no dispensing fee.

How much the dispensing fee is

The dispensing fee at a Private Participating Pharmacy is $7, $10 or $14 per item, depending on the drug (rates set on 1 April 2024).

Not all items are free. You may be required to pay for some items at both government and private pharmacies, for example if:

  • the item is a contraceptive
  • the item is a repeat of certain medicines, such as allergy medicines
  • two drugs fall into the same therapeutic category
  • the quantity exceeds the maximum allowed free by the BDS

At the pharmacy

  1. Hand your prescription and ID documents to the pharmacist.
  2. The pharmacist will check your eligibility against the Barbados Drug Service register.
  3. If you are eligible, you get your medication and sign a form so the BDS can reimburse the pharmacy.
  4. At a Private Participating Pharmacy you pay the dispensing fee. You may also be required to pay for some items at both government and private pharmacies (see What you pay).

If your documents are not accepted, do not pay full price. Ask the pharmacist to call the Barbados Drug Service on (246) 535-4300 first.

If something goes wrong

My drug is not on the formulary
Ask your doctor about a therapeutically equivalent alternative on the Barbados National Drug Formulary. If no equivalent exists, your doctor can request a Category B exception through the Drug and Therapeutics Committee at Queen Elizabeth Hospital, or through the Barbados Drug Service Director. This only applies in the government setting. The BDS does not honour Category B requests from private doctors.
My ID was not accepted
Ask the pharmacist to contact BDS on (246) 535-4300 before you pay. If your permanent residency documentation needs updating, contact the Immigration Department at BTI Corporate Centre, Princess Alice Highway, Bridgetown BB11093, St. Michael.
I was charged full price but think I qualify
Keep your receipt. Contact BDS on (246) 535-4300 or email management@drugservice.gov.bb with your prescription, receipt and ID documents. You may be entitled to a refund.
How are dispensing fees and prices set?
The dispensing fee paid by the patient is contractually set, and all PPPs are bound to charge patients according to this pricing schedule for items dispensed to beneficiaries under the SBS program. The same pricing schedule applies in government pharmacies. Non-formulary items, and formulary items dispensed to non-beneficiaries, are priced according to each pharmacy’s own business model.
My child just turned 16
The under-16 benefit ends on the child's 16th birthday. If the child has a qualifying chronic condition (asthma, diabetes, etc.), the benefit continues under that category, because the drug and the patient's age determine qualification.
The pharmacy is out of stock
The pharmacy should direct you to another participating pharmacy. If they cannot help, call BDS on (246) 535-4300.
Collecting on someone else's behalf
Medication must be collected in person. This service is not available online. Someone else can collect for you. They will need to bring your prescription, your ID, your permanent residency documents, and their own ID.
