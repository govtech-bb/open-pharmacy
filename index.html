Get free or subsidised medication

Use this service to find pharmacies where you can use the Special Benefit Service (SBS). SBS is a Barbados Drug Service programme that can lower or remove the cost of some prescription medicines.

You can search for:

  • government pharmacies
  • private pharmacies that work with the Barbados Drug Service
Search for a pharmacy

Who can get help with medicine costs

You can get help if you are a Barbadian citizen or permanent resident, or you are covered by CARICOM free movement.

One of these must also apply:

  • you are under 16
  • you are 65 or over
  • you are any age and need medicine for diabetes, asthma, high blood pressure, cancer, epilepsy or glaucoma

You will also need:

  • a prescription from a doctor registered with the Medical Council of Barbados — the doctor can work in the public or private health service
  • a prescription for a medicine on the Barbados National Drug Formulary

The Barbados National Drug Formulary is the list of medicines covered by the Barbados Drug Service.

Check if your medicine is listed

What if my medicine is not listed?

Ask your doctor:

  • if there is another listed medicine you can use
  • if they can ask for special approval

Special approval is only for government pharmacy use.

What you may pay

Some medicines may still cost money.

  • At a government pharmacy, there is no fee to prepare your medicine.
  • At a private pharmacy that works with the Barbados Drug Service, you pay a fee to prepare your medicine. This is called a dispensing fee. The amount depends on the medicine.

Prescription colour

Your prescription colour can affect which pharmacies can fill it.

Check what prescription colours mean

What to take to the pharmacy

  • the prescription
  • original accepted ID for the person the medicine is for

Accepted ID can be:

  • Barbados National Identification (ID) card
  • Barbados passport
  • Barbados driver's licence
  • child health book, if the medicine is for a child

Photocopies are not accepted.

Need help at the pharmacy?

You may need help if:

  • your ID is not accepted
  • the pharmacy asks you to pay more than expected
  • the pharmacy is out of stock

What to do if there is a problem at the pharmacy

You can also contact the Barbados Drug Service.

Phone: (246) 535-4300

Email: management@drugservice.gov.bb
